# Tailoring Instructions (Default)

> These are generic defaults. Place your own `instructions.md` in `cv/uploads/`
> to override with personal rules.

## General
- Keep resume to 1 page maximum
- Quantify achievements where possible
- Do not include references or hobbies

## Positioning Philosophy

The resume is not a project catalogue. It is a positioning document.
The goal is not to describe what was built — it is to show what kind of person built it.

When selecting and ordering content, ask: **"Does this show fit, not just ability?"**

### Project Selection
- Show at most 2–3 projects. Fewer, sharper projects beat a long list.
- Pick projects that directly mirror what the JD is asking for — not the most impressive ones overall.
- If a project is tangentially relevant, leave it out. White space is better than noise.

### Project Framing
- Lead each project with the outcome or the problem solved — not the tech stack.
- One bullet on what it does / what problem it solved.
- One bullet on the result or impact (quantified if possible).
- One bullet on the stack — brief, at the end, not the headline.
- Do NOT open a project bullet with "Built", "Developed", "Created", or "Implemented". These are weak verbs that describe activity, not value.
- Prefer: "Reduced", "Enabled", "Automated", "Replaced", "Eliminated", "Shipped", "Scaled".

### Experience Framing
- Lead with impact, not responsibility.
- "Owned X which led to Y" beats "Responsible for X".
- Avoid listing tools as achievements. Tools are context; outcomes are achievements.

### Skills Section
- Keep it tight. Only list skills directly relevant to the JD.
- Do not pad with skills the candidate barely knows — it dilutes the signal.

### Overall Narrative
- The resume should feel like it was written by someone who understands the role deeply,
  not someone who applied to 50 jobs this week.
- Every section should answer the implicit question: "Why are you the right person for THIS role?"
- Cut anything that doesn't answer that question.

## Skills — Selection Rules

> Skills are controlled at two levels: **categories** (include/exclude entire categories)
> and **individual items** (include/exclude specific skills within a category).

### How it works
1. **Category-level:** The system selects which skill categories to include based on JD
   relevance and your instructions. Irrelevant categories are excluded entirely.
2. **Item-level:** Within included categories, individual skills can be excluded if
   they are not relevant to the JD or if your instructions say to remove them.
3. **Ordering:** Categories are ordered by relevance to the JD. You can pin specific
   categories to fixed positions (e.g., "Programming always at the bottom").

### Master CV formatting
- Structure skills as one `\textbf{Category:}` per `\item` line
- Each category should list its skills as comma-separated items
- The system parses categories from `\textbf{Name:}` patterns — keep this format consistent
